## Overview
This repository contains the foundational manuscript and source files for the **Null-Directional Kinematics (NDK)** framework. 

Despite their empirical success, General Relativity and Quantum Mechanics remain conceptually incompatible. This paper proposes a unified reformulation where **invariant null propagation at the speed of light (c) constitutes the fundamental kinematic substrate of the universe.** In this framework, spacetime, gravity, inertia, and quantum uncertainty are not fundamental postulates, but emergent, relational phenomena arising from the statistical resolution of directional indeterminacy.

## Core Physical Insights

**1. Gravity as Spacetime Flow (Not Intrinsic Curvature)**
Rather than treating the equivalence principle as an expression of intrinsic geometric curvature, NDK treats it as the material acceleration of the spacetime flow itself. The gravitational field emerges as the convective transport of the substrate. This exactly recovers Newtonian gravity in the weak-field limit and reproduces General Relativity expressed in Gullstrand-Painlevé coordinates.

**2. Quantum Uncertainty as Directional Indeterminacy**
Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle is reinterpreted from a mysterious quantum postulate into a strict geometric constraint. Squeezing the spatial location of a particle mathematically requires utilizing a broader spread of unresolved propagation directions on the local celestial sphere.

**3. Emergent Relativistic Kinematics**
Physical states are described by unresolved null-directional components. The familiar Lorentz factor emerges naturally as the inverse of the unresolved fraction of this null propagation capacity.
